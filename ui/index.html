Quiet Time Schedule

Automatically enable Quiet Time on a daily schedule. When the window opens the gateway blanks the display and stops moving flaps for normal updates. The display is restored when the window ends. Times are in your browser's local time zone.

Module Wall

Common panel & wall combinations. Choosing one fills the LED Panel card and the rows and columns below, and saves them. Reboot (power-cycle) to apply.

The wall of virtual split-flap modules drawn on the LED panel. One module per cell, IDs assigned left-to-right, top-to-bottom (module 0 = top-left). This also lays out the Live Display on the Display tab. Changing it creates or destroys modules — reboot to apply.

LED Panel

HUB75 geometry and reel speed. Width, height and color depth are read once at boot — reboot to apply. Brightness and the flip settings take effect on the next frame. Width is the whole chain: two 64×32 panels in series are 128×32.

Tick this if red comes out blue. HUB75 panels vary: a BGR panel draws red as blue, blue as orange and yellow as cyan, while green and white look perfectly normal — which is why it is easy to miss. Takes effect immediately.

Serial Debug

Enable verbose serial output on the native USB serial port (115200 baud). Shows every RX/TX frame, MQTT events, and module activity.

Home Assistant

Publish MQTT auto-discovery so the gateway appears in Home Assistant as a device with a display text control, a Quiet Time switch, and diagnostic sensors. Requires MQTT to be configured. Leave off to avoid publishing discovery/state topics you don't need.
